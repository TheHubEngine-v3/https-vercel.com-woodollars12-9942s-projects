LESSON 1 · FOUNDATION
What Is Video Studio?
Understand the 3 video systems and how they connect
›
Hub Engine v4 has THREE video systems:
SYSTEM 1 — AUTO-PUBLISH 🚀
The Fully Automated Pipeline
You type one idea → Becky writes the script → ElevenLabs records the voice → FreeStack Video builds the video → it posts to YouTube automatically. One button does everything.
Best for: Daily content · Podcast episodes · Affiliate promos
SYSTEM 2 — VIDEO STUDIO 🎬
The Manual Builder (What This Course Teaches)
You control every step. Choose a template, write or have Becky write your script, build scenes, set timing, then render. Good for customer videos, longer projects, and learning how it all works.

Build avatar-based videos with your cast — Lisa, Susan, Paul, Alisha, Becky Host, or Becky Guest. Choose their style, write a script, generate voice, render. Works inside Video Studio or Auto-Publish.

LESSON 2 · TEMPLATES
Choosing the Right Template
What each template does and when to use it
›
Video Studio has 4 templates. Each one pre-loads a set of scenes in the right order for that type of video. Pick the one that matches what you are making.
📣
Promotional Template
6 scenes · Best for sales and promo videos
Structure: Hook → Problem → Solution → Demo → Benefits → CTA. Designed to sell something or promote a service. Use this when you want someone to take action after watching.
Scene 1: Opening Hook (60 sec)
Scene 2: Problem Statement (60 sec)
Scene 3: Solution Introduction (90 sec)
Scene 4: Demo or Proof (90 sec)
Scene 5: Benefits (60 sec)
Scene 6: Call to Action (60 sec)
📖
Explainer Template
8 scenes · Best for how-to and educational videos
Structure: Title → Overview → Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Results → Summary → CTA. Use this when you are teaching something or walking someone through a process.
Scenes 1–2: Setup the topic (90 sec total)
Scenes 3–5: Three main steps (225 sec total)
Scenes 6–7: Results and recap (105 sec total)
Scene 8: Call to Action (60 sec)
🛍
Showcase Template
7 scenes · Best for products and service features
Structure: Introduction → Feature 1 → Feature 2 → Live Demo → Customer Benefits → Pricing and Offer → CTA. Use when you want to show what something does in detail.
➕
Blank Template
5 empty scenes · Build your own structure
Starts with 5 blank scenes. You name them and set the timing. Best when you already know exactly what you want to say and just need a container to put it in.
💡 Quick Decision Guide — Which Template Should I Pick?
→ Making a video to sell something? Use Promotional
→ Teaching or explaining how something works? Use Explainer
→ Showing what a product or service does? Use Showcase
→ Already have a plan in your head? Use Blank
→ Not sure? Start with Promotional — it works for almost everything.

LESSON 3 · SCRIPT & SCENES
Writing Your Script and Building Scenes
How to write naturally, use Becky, and set timing right
›
Part A — Writing the Script
Option 1 — Write It Yourself
Go to the Script tab. Type in the big text box. Keep it conversational — write the way you talk, not the way you type a formal letter. Read it out loud while you write. If it sounds awkward when you say it, rewrite it.
Option 2 — Ask Becky to Write It ⭐ Recommended
Type one sentence describing your video in the Becky brief field. Be specific. The more detail you give her, the better the script will be. Then tap Becky — Write My Script.
Good brief examples:
✅ "2-minute video about how AI tools help beginners make money online with affiliate marketing"
✅ "1-minute promo for Smith Roofing in Orange Texas, storm damage repair, free estimates, local family business"
❌ "make me a video" — Too vague. Becky needs details to write well.
💡 Word Count and Timing
Speaking pace is about 150 words per minute. Use this to plan your script length:
1 minute video~150 words
2 minute video~300 words
3 minute video~450 words
5 minute video~750 words
The word count display updates live as you type so you can track your progress.
Part B — Building Scenes
Scenes are the individual parts of your video. Each scene has a name and a duration in seconds. They add up to your total video length. The progress bar turns green when you hit your target.
How Scenes Work
When you pick a template the scenes auto-load. Each one has a default time. You can rename them, adjust the seconds, add content notes, or delete and add new ones. FreeStack Video uses the scene list to know how long each section of your video runs.
Scene Timing Tips
Keep your hook scene short — 30 to 45 seconds. Middle scenes 60 to 90 seconds. CTA at the end 30 to 45 seconds. For a 1-minute test video use 3 scenes at 20 seconds each.
⚠️ Common Mistake to Avoid
Do not leave scenes at 0 seconds. Every scene must have a duration or the render will fail. If you are not sure, set each scene to 60 seconds and adjust from there.

LESSON 4 · RENDERING
How to Render and Get Your Video URL
Step-by-step through the render tab, status tracker, and result
›
Rendering is the step where your script and scenes get turned into an actual video file. FreeStack Video does this for you — it takes 1 to 3 minutes. Here is exactly what to do.
1️⃣
Check the Pre-Render Summary
When you tap the Render tab, you will see a summary card showing your project name, client, template, scene count, and total duration. There is a green or red status bar at the bottom. If it is red, go back and fix the issue before tapping render.
2️⃣
Make Sure FreeStack Video Key Is Saved
FreeStack Video needs no API key. It is your own server running on Render.com. Make sure your FreeStack Video URL is saved in Settings and the server is deployed.
FreeStack Video URL: https://freestack-video.onrender.com
3️⃣
Tap 🎬 Start Render
The button turns to "⏳ Rendering..." and three status steps appear: Building Timeline → Rendering Video → Video Ready. Each one updates live as FreeStack Video processes your project. Do not close the app or navigate away while rendering.
4️⃣
Render Status Explained
The status checker polls FreeStack Video every 10 seconds up to 30 times (5 minutes max). You will see the render ID, current status, and check count update in real time. Most renders finish in 1 to 3 minutes. No watermark on your videos.
⏳ Running
= FreeStack Video is processing your project
✅ Done
= That step completed successfully
❌ Failed
= Something went wrong — check the error message
5️⃣
Get Your Video URL
When the render completes, a green result box appears with your video URL. Tap 📋 Copy URL to copy it to clipboard. Tap ⬇ Open Video to preview it in a new tab. No watermark — FreeStack Video is your own server. Download and share directly.
🔧 Troubleshooting — If the Render Fails
Error: Server offline → FreeStack Video may be cold-starting. Wait 30 sec and retry.
Error: Duration too short → Go back to Scenes and add more time to your scenes
Error: No scenes → You skipped the Scenes tab — go back and confirm scenes loaded
Render timed out → Wait 1 minute, tap Retry — FreeStack Video is processing on free-tier CPU
Render failed: unknown → Your free sandbox may have hit the 20-minute/month limit

LESSON 5 · SAVE & NEXT STEPS
Saving, Sharing, and What Comes Next
History, Avatar Creator, and the road to Auto-Publish
›
After a Successful Render — 4 Actions
📋
Copy the URL
Tap Copy URL in the render result box. The link goes to your clipboard. You can paste it into WhatsApp, email, or any app to share or preview it.
💾
Save to Project History
Go to the Save tab and tap Save Project to History. This stores the project name, video URL, date, and scene count on your device. You can come back and find it anytime.
📋
Duplicate for Next Video
Tap Duplicate to copy the current project with "(Copy)" added to the name. Edit the title and script, then render again. This is the fastest way to produce multiple videos from one template.
🔄
Re-Render After Changes
Go back to any earlier tab, make your changes, then tap Render again. The system will produce a new URL. Previous URL still works. Save both if you need to compare.
