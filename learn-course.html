Send your guest the recording info and script outline. Check all your equipment — microphone working, camera or phone charged, lighting set up, headphones ready. Test everything before you record. No surprises on recording day.

🎥 STEP 2 — PRODUCTION
DAY 23
Record Your Video + Audio
Use Vmake Studio to generate your avatar clips OR record your own video footage. For AI avatar: open Vmake → upload host photo → paste Becky's script → generate each [CLIP BREAK] segment. For self-recorded: use good camera, external mic, proper lighting. Record audio separately if possible for better quality.
🎬 RECORD CLIPS
DAY 24
Lighting + Setup for Best Video Quality
Learn proper lighting for talking head videos. Soft front light removes shadows. Avoid windows behind you. Even a simple ring light or lamp in front makes a huge difference. Practice your setup and take a test recording before your real session.
💡 LIGHTING PRACTICE
✂️ STEP 3 — POST-PRODUCTION
DAY 25
Join Your Clips in Vmake
Open Vmake.ai → import all your generated clips → arrange in order: Hook → Script segments → CTA. Use Vmake's assembly tools to join them into one complete video. Check the flow — does it tell a clear story from start to finish?
🔗 JOIN CLIPS IN VMAKE
DAY 26
Audio Editing + Video Sync
Check your audio quality — remove background noise, adjust volume levels so the host is clear and consistent. For Vmake AI avatar videos the lip sync is automatic. For self-recorded: use Audacity (free) to clean the audio. Make sure audio and video stay in sync throughout the whole episode.
🔊 AUDIO CLEANUP
🖼️ STEP 4 — THUMBNAIL + METADATA
DAY 27
Create Your Thumbnail
Use Vmake's AI Thumbnail tool OR Canva to create an eye-catching thumbnail. Include your host's face, bold text with the episode topic, and your Hub Engine v3 brand colors. Good thumbnails increase clicks by 50% on YouTube. Make it clear what the episode is about in one glance.
🖼️ DESIGN THUMBNAIL
DAY 28
Write Tags, Description + Closed Captions
Use Becky's show notes from Vmake Studio as your video description — it is already written! Add 8-10 relevant tags for your topic. Enable auto-captions on YouTube or use Vmake's caption tool. Captions help deaf viewers AND boost SEO so more people find your video.
🏷️ ADD TAGS + CAPTIONS
